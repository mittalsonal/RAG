Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a method that helps an AI model give better answers. Before generating a response, the model looks for information from reliable sources outside its training data, such as databases or documents.

Large Language Models (LLMs) are trained on huge amounts of data and use many parameters to create responses for tasks like answering questions, translating languages, or finishing sentences.

RAG improves these models by allowing them to use extra, up-to-date knowledge from specific areas or an organization’s internal information without retraining the model. This makes the AI’s answers more accurate, relevant, and useful, while also being cost-effective.
